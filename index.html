Projects

Arduino-Powered Line Follower Bot

Designed and built an autonomous Line Follower Robot capable of detecting path boundaries and navigating based on surface contrast.

  • Tested and calibrated 6 Infrared (IR) sensors to accurately detect line contrast and generate reliable real-time input signals.

  • Programmed an Arduino UNO to process sensor inputs and implement decision logic for forward, left, and right movements.

  • Integrated Arduino output signals with dual DC motors to achieve smooth, logic-driven navigation.

  • Designed and implemented the complete control logic using the Arduino programming language for autonomous path following.

  • Powered the robot using lithium-ion batteries for stable voltage supply and consistent motor/microcontroller performance.

  • Secured 2nd place in EXPRO’17 for demonstrating robust autonomous tracking and hardware integration.

Automated Intrusion Detection System

Developed an Automated Intrusion Detection System using PIR sensors, image processing, and GSM alerts to enhance security in sensitive zones.

  • Deployed Passive Infrared (PIR) sensors for continuous monitoring and real-time detection of human presence or motion.

  • Trained a baseline environmental model using Arduino-driven sensor readings to identify anomalies and unauthorized intrusions.

  • Implemented an OpenCV-based image processing pipeline to detect abnormal movement patterns beyond the trained baseline.

  • Integrated GSM modules to instantly send intrusion alerts to nearby control stations, ensuring rapid response.

  • Engineered a non-lethal chloroform-based spraying mechanism to automatically neutralize intruders when a threat was confirmed.

Work Experience

My professional journey began with a strong focus on cybersecurity fundamentals and hands-on technical exploration. I worked extensively on building Proof of Concepts (PoCs) for several OWASP Top 10 vulnerabilities, gaining deep practical exposure to how real-world exploits work and how they can be effectively mitigated. This phase helped me build a solid foundation in application security and threat modeling.

In parallel, I developed a strong understanding of networking principles, including endpoint discovery and device visibility. I actively worked on discovering endpoints within enterprise networks using Nmap OS discovery and ARP table analysis, helping identify both managed and unmanaged assets across subnets.

I later contributed to the design and development of a Firmware Vulnerability Management feature for network devices. This included identifying vulnerable firmware versions, correlating them with advisories, and supporting firmware deployment workflows to remediate security risks across network infrastructure.

As my role evolved, I transitioned into a Product Management position, where I began translating complex security challenges into scalable, customer-centric solutions. I played a key role in designing features such as:

  • Misconfiguration Reverts – enabling administrators to roll back risky or non-compliant configurations
  • System Quarantine & Compliance Enforcement – isolating non-compliant endpoints to reduce organizational risk
  • Drive-level Vulnerability Detection – identifying security risks at the storage and drive layer of endpoints

I also designed and implemented workflows for automated patch deployment integrated with ServiceNow, where patch actions were triggered only after an approved Change Request, ensuring compliance with enterprise ITSM and audit requirements.

A significant part of my role involved working closely with customers. I regularly analyzed customer feedback, lead-loss data, and inputs from the Feature Request Management Tool to understand real-world pain points and refine product direction.

On a recurring basis, I prepared monthly presentations to review customer-requested features, product gaps, and upcoming enhancements. Additionally, I contributed to year-on-year roadmap planning, presenting long-term feature strategies and enhancements aligned with business goals and evolving security landscapes.

Overall, my experience spans both deep technical implementation and strategic product design, allowing me to bridge the gap between engineering, security, and customer needs.
