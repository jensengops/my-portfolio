Projects

Arduino Powered Line Follower Bot

Designed and built an autonomous Line Follower Robot capable of detecting path boundaries and navigating based on surface contrast.

  • Tested and calibrated 6 Infrared (IR) sensors to accurately detect line contrast and generate reliable real-time input signals.
  • Programmed an Arduino UNO to process sensor inputs and implement decision logic for forward, left, and right movements.
  • Integrated Arduino output signals with dual DC motors to achieve smooth, logic-driven navigation.
  • Designed and implemented the complete control logic using the Arduino programming language for autonomous path following.
  • Powered the robot using lithium-ion batteries for stable voltage supply and consistent motor/microcontroller performance.
  • Secured 2nd place in EXPRO’17 for demonstrating robust autonomous tracking and hardware integration.

Automated Intrusion Detection System

Developed an Automated Intrusion Detection System using PIR sensors, image processing, and GSM alerts to enhance security in sensitive zones.

  • Deployed Passive Infrared (PIR) sensors for continuous monitoring and real-time detection of human presence or motion.
  • Trained a baseline environmental model using Arduino-driven sensor readings to identify anomalies and unauthorized intrusions.
  • Implemented an OpenCV-based image processing pipeline to detect abnormal movement patterns beyond the trained baseline.
  • Integrated GSM modules to instantly send intrusion alerts to nearby control stations, ensuring rapid response.
  • Engineered a non-lethal chloroform-based spraying mechanism to automatically neutralize intruders when a threat was confirmed.

Work Experience

My professional journey started with hands-on exploration of cybersecurity fundamentals, where I built multiple Proof of Concepts for OWASP Top 10 vulnerabilities. This experience gave me a strong understanding of how real-world attacks work and how they can be mitigated at scale.

Alongside application security, I worked extensively on networking concepts such as endpoint visibility and asset discovery. Using Nmap OS detection and ARP table analysis, I helped identify managed and unmanaged devices across enterprise networks.

I later contributed to the development of firmware vulnerability management for network devices. This involved tracking vulnerable firmware versions, mapping them to advisories, and supporting firmware deployment workflows to reduce infrastructure risk.

As my role evolved, I transitioned into product management, focusing on transforming complex security problems into practical, customer-focused features. I played a key role in designing:

  • Configuration rollback mechanisms to revert risky or non-compliant changes
  • System quarantine workflows to isolate non-compliant endpoints
  • Drive-level vulnerability detection to uncover risks at the storage layer

I also worked on automating patch deployments through ServiceNow, where deployments were executed only after approved change requests, ensuring alignment with enterprise ITSM and audit practices.

Customer collaboration was a core part of my role. I regularly analyzed feedback, feature requests, and lead-loss data to understand customer pain points and guide product decisions.

On a recurring basis, I prepared monthly reviews to track requested features and enhancements, and contributed to year-on-year roadmap planning by presenting long-term product direction aligned with business and security needs.

Overall, my experience spans hands-on security engineering and strategic product ownership, allowing me to effectively bridge the gap between technology, users, and business outcomes.
